The interesting result isn't a single number — it's the shape of two curves and what the interconnect is doing underneath them. Three findings drive everything below: decode stays remarkably flat as context grows (hybrid linear attention), prefill is the real long-context cost and it's latency-bound on collective sync, not bandwidth, and MTP roughly doubles decode for a ~6% prefill tax — a trade a coding workload should always take.

Setup

Hardware. 4× RTX 5060 Ti (16 GB each, Blackwell sm_120), PCIe Gen4 ×8, no NVLink and no P2P (chipset-disabled) — cross-GPU traffic host-staged. Vast.ai container, driver 580.95, CUDA 13.0.

Model & engine. Qwen3.6-27B (hybrid linear/full attention + MTP head), Unsloth Q8_0 GGUF, served by current llama.cpp in 4-way --split-mode tensor, flash attention on, f16 KV (no quant). MTP via --spec-type draft-mtp (heads embedded in the same weights).

Method. Identical fixed-length coding prompts (real source corpus + an implement-this-function task), streamed over the OpenAI API. PP = prompt tokens ÷ time-to-first-token (cold prefill, prompt cache off); TG = decode tok/s over 256 generated tokens at bs=1.

What the interconnect is actually doing

The flat ~800 tok/s prefill ceiling is modest for four GPUs, so the obvious suspect is the no-P2P PCIe link. Live nvidia-smi dmon during a 262k run says otherwise — the bus is only ~15–20% utilized, but SM occupancy keeps collapsing mid-collective:

GPU SM% rxpci txpci (MB/s, PCIe Gen4 x8 ≈ 16 GB/s) 1 23 1829 1657 ← stalled while the bus is busy 1 4 1551 1691 ← 4% — idle, waiting on sync 2 90 977 2762 3 95 1228 615

So it's not bandwidth — it's latency. At bs=1 every layer does a tiny all-reduce that sits on the critical path; the fixed per-collective cost (kernel launch + sync + a small host-staged hop) dominates, and compute idles waiting for it.

Cross-box confirmation: P2P doesn't help
On a sister 2-card rig, enabling P2P via the Geohot/reBAR patch produced identical harness numbers. That's the clean proof: P2P is a bandwidth optimization, and this workload isn't bandwidth-bound. For single-stream inference, chase fewer/cheaper sync points — not P2P.

The cards are never the wall

Independent confirmation from the power rail: during a live 262k prefill the GPUs draw ~100–113 W against a 160 W limit at 60 °C — even while utilization.gpu reads 100%. Power is the honest proxy for real compute intensity, and at ~65% of limit it says the SMs are stalling inside their kernels, not doing full-throughput math. No power throttle, no thermal throttle, ~60 W of headroom per card unused — at bs=1 the hardware is structurally under-fed. That's four independent signals — SM dips, idle PCIe, the P2P-null A/B, and low power — pointing at one cause.

Takeaways

  • 1262k context is real on 64 GB, unquantized. Q8_0 weights + f16 KV land at ~11.6 GB/card at full context — hybrid linear attention keeps KV tiny. No KV quantization needed.
  • 2Turn MTP on for coding. ~2× decode (1.7–2.3×) for a ~6% prefill tax. You generate far more than a couple tokens per turn, so it's a clear win.
  • 3Prefill, not decode, is the long-context cost. Decode barely moves; TTFT grows linearly with context. Optimize prompt reuse / caching in the harness, not decode.
  • 4The limiter is collective latency, not the interconnect. PCIe sits idle; GPUs stall on per-layer sync. P2P/reBAR won't move it — confirmed by a 2-card A/B.

Caveats. Single box, single stream (bs=1); throughput/serving is a different regime. Prefill measured cold (cache off) to isolate the scaling curve — a real harness with prompt caching sees far lower effective TTFT. The 262,144 point is the model's trained maximum, so it leaves almost no room to decode; the near-max throughput point is measured at ~255k so 256 decode tokens fit inside the ceiling. Numbers are medians of repeated runs.
